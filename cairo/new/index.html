Proportional searches since 2004 worldwide by category

Any change in searches for rentals since 2004?

Three animations are presented to compare changes from 2004 to 2018 across each rental category. Searches in all rental categories reached their peak in 2004 and fluctuated until 2018. These downward trends might suggest that people have increasing disposable incomes and they prefer to buy commodities instead of renting them.

Searches in car rental went down sharply from 2004 to 2015, then increased a bit afterwards. The increase was probably due to the popularity of self-driving travel in the past couple of years. Searches in house rentals increased significantly since 2012 after a downward trend. In 2018, searches in apartment and equipment rentals are the same. No big difference in the last three rental categories (i.e., company, uniform, and jewelry) can be identified.

What the Geographical Distribution Looks Like In U.S.?

An overview of search distribution in the U.S. is shown in the map below. The search index in car rental is high in popular tourist states such as Nevada, Hawaii, Alaska and Florida. Texas has the higher search index in house, while New York and surrounding states have high search indexes in apartment rental and jewelry rental. Northern Midwest has high search index in uniform rental. With regard to equipment and company rental, one single area or state seems to be dominating.

What the Seasonal Pattern Looks Like Worldwide?

Seven rental categories are shown separately with different seasonal patterns. Colored lines are the average monthly search indexes, while grey lines represent the data from 2004 to 2018. For the car rental, the search index is high from May to September, probably because the summer holiday during which families travel around. The search index in apartment reaches its peak in June, and that in uniform sharply increases in December. In terms of jewelry, the search index increases significantly in March.
